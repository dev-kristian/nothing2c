### My Friends' Movie Nights Were a Scheduling Nightmare, So I Built an App to Fix It

Hey everyone,

You know that feeling? The group chat for "Movie Night!" pops up, and you know what's coming. A flood of messages, a dozen different time suggestions, and the inevitable "What are we even watching?"

I love watching movies with my friends, but honestly, I was starting to dread the planning part more than I was looking forward to the movie itself. It was always a mess of "I can't do Friday," "What about Saturday afternoon?" and the eternal "Seen it." We were spending more time trying to agree on a plan than actually watching anything.

I'm a developer, so my brain is wired to find solutions for this kind of stuff. I thought, "There has to be a better way." That's how my project, Kinonchill, was born.

It started as a simple tool to solve my own problem. The goal was to kill the annoying parts of planning a watch party.

Here’s how it works:

Instead of a chaotic group chat, you just create a "Session" in the app and invite your friends.

For scheduling, everyone just marks when they're free on a shared calendar. The app instantly shows the best time slots that work for everyone. No more back-and-forth.

For picking a movie, we just run a poll. Everyone can throw in a few suggestions, and we all vote. The movie with the most votes wins. Simple, democratic, and drama-free. It also lets you see your friends' watchlists, so you can suggest something you know they'll like.

And when you're just looking to watch something on your own, you can do that too. I've embedded a few streaming providers, so you can watch a movie or show right on the site without having to jump to another service.

For the nerds in the room, I built it with Next.js and Tailwind CSS on the front end, and Firebase for all the backend stuff like the database and user accounts. The movie info comes from the TMDb API.

It started as a weekend project just for my friends and me, but I'm hoping it can help other people escape their own scheduling nightmares. I'd love for you to check it out and let me know what you think.
